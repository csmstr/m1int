Problem Solving


Instructor(s)

Jean-Charles Régin

Description

  • 2 ECTS

The objective of this course is to show that computers may be used for solving exactly complex combinatorial problems, like rostering, bin packing or car sequencing …

  • First, some famous greedy algorithms will be introduced or recalled in order to show their limits, notably that, in general, they do not compute the optimal solution.
  • Then, the exhaustive enumeration will be introduced as a generalisation of greedy algorithms. The students will discover the power of these algorithms but also their main drawback: their exponential complexity.
  • At last, some techniques will be introduced to reduce the search space that need to be traversed for solving a problem.

Prerequisites

  • Standard Algorithms (sort, tree traversal, graph algorithms…)
  • Standard Data Structures (array, list, heap)

Others

  • Number of lectures (in hrs): 12
  • Number of Exercise Classes (in hrs): 9
